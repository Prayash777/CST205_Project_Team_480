History of Minimalist Interior Design

Minimalist interior design finds its roots in the early 20th century, with pioneers like the German Bauhaus movement and architects like Ludwig Mies van der Rohe.
These designers championed clean lines, functional furniture, and the idea that form should follow function. The movement gained momentum in the mid-20th century, with artists like Donald Judd and architects like John Pawson pushing the boundaries of minimalism.
